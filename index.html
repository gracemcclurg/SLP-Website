SLP Pal




SLP Pal is a Speech Language Pathology (SLP) tool developed to help individuals practice forming a variety of speech sounds through vocabulary words.
Each of the vocab words have a paired recording of Annemarie McClurg, a Communication Disorders major at Western Kentucky University, modeling common speech sounds with physical cues. Users can click the check box on each vocab word to indicate that they practiced their pronunciation for the day. This will automatically update the weekly habit tracker where they can monitor their progress!

About


Intro to SLP


Speech-language pathology (SLP) is a field of study dedicated to helping individuals with speech, language, voice, and swallowing impairments. Speech-language pathologists can help these individuals through diagnosis, developing treatment plans, providing therapy, and helping track and evaluate their progress.



About SLP Pal


SLP Pal enables users to practice their pronunciation of commonly mispronounced speech sounds. This interactive web tool allows users to view videos of vocabulary words paired with hand cues. Hand cues are used in therapy to remind patients of the physical attributes needed to pronounce the vocabulary.

For example, when working on the /h/ sound associated with the word ‘hug’, the paired video shows a woman blowing hot air on her hand. Because hot air is needed to make the /h/ sound, pairing this action with your pronunciation reinforces the physical element of pronouncing the word.
Another example would be the /n/ sound associated with the word ‘nail’. This sound is a nasal sound, meaning to pronounce it, one must work with their nose. To remind the user of this, the vocab video shows a woman tapping her nose.
Having the motions paired with the pronunciation allows users to learn about these helpful tools and to implement them into their own therapy journey.

Another aspect of SLP pal is a habit tracker that allows users to view their weekly progress. This is impactful for helping users create a routine that helps build their abilities over time.

Behind The Creation of SLP Pal


SLP Pal was created by sisters Grace and Annemarie McClurg. Annemarie is a student at Western Kentucky University studying Communication Disorders and Grace is a student at the University of Louisville studying Computer Science and Engineering. This project allowed us to both learn about the other's field while creating an impactful tool for SLP.

Annemarie learned more about web development with concepts such as local storage that enables users to see their weekly progress, JavaScript that supports interactivity, HTML, and CSS. Grace learned about SLP concepts including hand cues and commonly mispronounced speech sounds.

This project has ultimately given us a more in-depth understanding of making accessible technology that helps empower others.
